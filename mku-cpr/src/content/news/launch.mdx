The Mount Kenya University Centre for Placenta Research opened today,
following the award of a £2,000,000 Wellcome Trust Discovery Award to a
consortium led by Prof. Jesse Gitaka. The five-year programme convenes MKU
(host), the University of Cambridge, the London School of Hygiene & Tropical
Medicine and the Max Delbrück Center for Molecular Medicine in Berlin.

The Centre is hosted within the existing MKU Centre for Malaria Elimination
at Thika and operates field offices in Bungoma, Busia, Homa Bay and Kiambu
counties. Its scientific brief covers placental malaria, congenital syphilis,
rubella, environmental exposures, and the broader determinants of
miscarriage, stillbirth, preterm birth and low birth weight.

> "This award represents a watershed moment, shifting the epicentre of
> discovery to the front lines of the crisis."
> — Prof. Jesse Gitaka

The Centre's stated three-year target is at least one diagnostic candidate
in field validation.
