## What we are doing

Routine peripheral-blood tests miss most placental *Plasmodium falciparum*
infections — the parasite sequesters in the intervillous space and is often
absent from the maternal circulation by delivery. We combine histopathology,
multiplex immunohistochemistry and proteomics on placental tissue collected
at delivery to map infection at the cellular level.

## Why it matters

Diagnostic blindness at the placenta means the women most at risk for
adverse pregnancy outcomes — stillbirth, preterm birth, low birth weight —
are the women routine antenatal screening fails first.
