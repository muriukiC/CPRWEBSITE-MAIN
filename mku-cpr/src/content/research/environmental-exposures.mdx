## What we are doing

Pregnancies in Bungoma, Busia and Homa Bay carry exposures that are
under-represented in the global literature on placental health: biomass
cookstoves, agricultural pyrethroids, and contaminated water sources. We
quantify those exposures at the individual-pregnancy level and link them to
placental histology and birth outcomes.

## Why it matters

Causal claims about pollutants and pregnancy require the pollutants to be
measured in the same population whose pregnancies are studied. Borrowed
exposure estimates from elsewhere are not enough.
