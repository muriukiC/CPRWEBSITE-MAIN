# Discovering, diagnosing and dismantling the diseases of pregnancy.

The MKU Centre for Placenta Research is the first centre in sub-Saharan Africa
dedicated to placental and pregnancy-disease discovery. We work where the
burden is highest — across Bungoma, Busia, Homa Bay and Kiambu counties in
Kenya — and partner with Cambridge, the London School of Hygiene & Tropical
Medicine and the Max Delbrück Center in Berlin.

## Why this matters

- Every year, malaria in pregnancy is estimated to contribute to **10,000 maternal deaths**.[^1]
- **200,000 stillbirths** are linked to placental disease.[^2]
- **500,000 babies** are born too small.[^2]

The science of pregnancy disease has historically been done far from where
pregnancy disease is. We are changing that.

[^1]: Desai M, et al. Lancet Infect Dis. 2007;7(2):93-104.
[^2]: Lawn JE, et al. Nat Rev Microbiol. 2018.
