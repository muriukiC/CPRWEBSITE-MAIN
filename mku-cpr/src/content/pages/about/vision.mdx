# A pregnancy-disease centre, built where pregnancy disease is.

Most of the world's stillbirths happen here. Most of the discovery should too.

The science of placental disease has historically been conducted far from its
most affected populations. The MKU Centre for Placenta Research was
established in January 2026 with a £2,000,000 Wellcome Trust Discovery Award
and operates across high-burden Kenyan counties — Bungoma, Busia, Homa Bay
and Kiambu — partnering with Cambridge, LSHTM and the Max Delbrück Center
in Berlin.

The Centre's scientific scope extends beyond malaria to encompass all major
placental threats: *Plasmodium falciparum*, *Treponema pallidum* (congenital
syphilis), rubella, and environmental exposures. We study these as one
integrated system at single-cell resolution, with a stated target for major
diagnostic breakthroughs within three years.
